General

After installation, many new admin/users ask, "Where do I go and what do I do next? While the GEDCOM standard which webtrees uses as a guideline for recording family data is very structured, there are still personal preferences to make about the rules and guidelines you intend to use to run your genealogy website and variations in data recording conventions. It is necessary for you to define these to have a smoothly operating site, but you may not yet have the experience or knowledge about the science to know what to do. Unfortunately, we can not possibly cover all of these variations nor attempt to impose our own styles upon you. Quite simply, you will have to learn by visiting other webtrees websites, reading this WIKI and learning administrative techniques.

Using or starting a family tree

As detailed in the installation article, you may now proceed to the Administration pages and using the Family tree link, upload your own family tree file (GEDCOM), import a replacement for the dummy file, or use the dummy file created to begin your new family tree.

Hint: When creating a new tree, remember that all of that tree's URL's will include a suffix description of "&ged=YourTreeName". It is recommended that you keep this tree name very short and do not use spaces or other special characters. You will be modifying the Family Tree title displayed in the header as you customize your family tree configuration file and the tree name created for upload has nothing to do with this display title, only the URL.

Additional recommendations

After activating a family tree, we would suggest you do the following:

  1. Study the webtrees configuration options (go through every part of the administration pages and try to understand their purpose). Use the extensive administration pages guide on this subject and the individual help statements next to each option to become educated on their purpose and effect on your site. This may take up to an hour or so, but it is time well spent.
  2. Decide on and configure your access and privacy rules. Use the extensive access and privacy guide on this subject and the individual help statements next to each option to become educated on their purpose and effect on your site.
  3. Begin considering how your site will be structured. Review other webtrees sites and look at their FAQ's, terms and conditions of use, their personal privacy policy and rules on republishing your data, and their data entry conventions (the formatting and style to use to record data). Then, use our Create Site FAQ guide to learn how to add all these details to your site. Advising your family members of your expectations can greatly enhance the frequency of their visits and quality of their contributions and is highly recommended.
  4. Review the quality of your family tree, particularly if you have started by uploading an existing family tree file (GEDCOM). We have an extensive article on family tree maintenance tips.
  5. You also want to think about how your site looks, especially to casual visitors.
    1. Have you changed the default site title that is displayed (removed the brackets and entered your preferred site name in Family Tree > Basics)?
    2. Is your site's layout appealing? Will it attract visitors? Does it encourage them to explore further?
    3. Can visitors and users contact you easily and do you respond quickly?
    4. Consider what languages should be available to visitors. You can save space on your server and simplify navigation and user education by limiting options to only those languages you intend to support.
    5. Decide which of several themes you wish to use and whether you will allow users to change themes. You can save space on your server and simplify navigation and user education by limiting options to only a few themes. Remember that, if you later choose only a custom theme, you will most likely need to install a standard theme prior to updating since the custom theme may not be compatible with the new webtrees code.

New versions of webtrees are released early and often. For many reasons, you should always upgrade promptly. Upgrading is easy, if you follow the WIKI instructions.

This list can go on and on and many of these decisions you will wish to make after you begin using your new website. There is no one way that is the right way, or the best way, but only your way.
