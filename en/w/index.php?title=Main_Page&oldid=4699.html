Genealogy

webtrees makes extensive use of the the near-universal standard for recording Family History known as a GEDCOM file.

GEDCOM was developed by the Family History Department of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints (LDS Church) to provide a flexible, uniform format for exchanging computerized genealogical data. GEDCOM is an acronym for GEnealogical Data COMmmunication. Its purpose is to foster the sharing of genealogical information and the development of a wide range of inter-operable software products to assist genealogists, historians, and other researchers.

The full specification can be viewed or downloaded from this link: GEDCOM Standard 5.5.1 (file size: 345 KB, MIME type: application/pdf)

Registration

Access to read any page on this WIKI does not need registration. To add or edit any entries does require registration, and is only available to registered members of webtrees.net who have some background of participation in the forum discussions. To register simply email the webtrees.net administrator, providing your webtrees.net user name and email address. Once verified you will be emailed a password for these WIKI pages (different to the one you have for webtrees.net). Due to time zone differences this process can take up to 24 hours, so please be patient. When you get that email please immediately update the password to one of your own preference, following the instructions in the email. We apologise for this inconvenience, but the level of spam we are experiencing here has forced us to implement this restriction.

Consult the User's Guide for information on using the wiki software.
