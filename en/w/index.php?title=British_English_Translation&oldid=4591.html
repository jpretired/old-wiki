Language Differences

The differences between US and UK English fall into a number of categories. These are all noted below.

Spelling

en-US en-GB Examples
-or -our behaviour, colour, favourite
-ize -ise authorise, computerise, customise, naturalise, personalise, recognise, romanise, standardise, synchronise, visualise
-er -re centre
-log -logue dialogue
-se -ce licence

Punctuation

en-US en-GB Notes
multi-media multimedia hyphens are used to disambiguate: "multiply" versus "multi-ply" or "third world-war" versus "third-world war"
birth, marriage, and death birth, marriage and death lists do not take a "serial comma" before the "and"
Don’t say “pub,” say “bar.” Don’t say ‘bar’, say ‘pub’. en-US puts punctuation inside the quotation marks, en-GB puts it outside. en-US prefers double quotation marks, en-GB prefers single quotation marks.

Vocabulary

en-US en-GB Notes
check box tick box to "check" a box means to confirm that it is a box
counter-clockwise anticlockwise
email e-mail it is pronounced "e-mail", not "em-ail"
forward slash slash the reverse of backslash is just slash
gender sex gender applies to grammar, sex applies to biology
persons people the plural of person is people
postal code post code

Cultural Differences

en-US en-GB Notes
example.com example.co.uk
John Doe Joe Bloggs A "dummy" person

Foreign Words

en-US en-GB Notes
Aland Islands Åland Islands British English retains accents on foreign words. American English only uses them for disambiguation, e.g. resume vs. résumé.

Misc

The following differences have been noted. These may simply be incorrect en-US strings

en-US en-GB
information of dead persons information about dead persons
