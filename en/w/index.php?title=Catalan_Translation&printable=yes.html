Introduction

This page tries to offer a common place to all the people interested in translating webtrees into Catalan.

It contains general translation guidelines, and the translation of some English terms that should be kept among all the translated phrases.

General translation guidelines

It's important that any translator read the Style Guide from Softcatalà. This document should be considered a reference document that should always be kept in mind. Under its rules a team of collaborators is updating and maintaining the Catalan versions for packages like Ubuntu, Fedora, Mozilla, OpenOffice, among others.

In general, you should adopt these rules:

  • General style. The style should be clear, logical and accurate. The reader should not feel that the text is a translation; to achieve this goal, you must avoid anglicisms (both lexical and syntactic), and ambiguous expressions and sentences long and complex.
  • You should avoid to catalanize foreign terms (eg. finestra emergent for pop-up window, paràmetre for setting, etc.). Check out this terminology list. Also, you can find help on this TERMCAT site.
  • Formality level. Often used in English language that tends to humanize the computer, using phrases such as Sorry, passwords do not match or please, select another option hich are totally alien to Catalan computer context. These expressions need to translate them as les contrasenyes no coincideixen and seleccioneu una altra opció.
  • Variation dialect. Whenever possible, and ensuring that the result does not sound forced, you should choose the most common forms around the speaking in the general area of translations. When in doubt (eg. certain verb endings), you should choose those general defined as the grammar of the Catalan language from Institut d'Estudis Catalans (Institute of Catalan Studies).
  • Gender neutralization. Always exists, opt for a more general construction (eg. Us donem la benvinguda for Welcome). When not possible, use common sense (eg. Pares for Parents, fills for children). We don't recommend the use of double forms (eg. Pares/mares, fills/es) except in those cases where the software needs explicitly indicate that unknown referred person's gender.
  • User vs. computer dialogue. Given the low precision of English in terms of verbal forms, you never know if the originals wanted to indicate an infinitive, imperative, indicative, etc. That is why it is necessary to establish rules of writing that are:
    • The user addresses the computer: Always use the second person singular imperative (which corresponds to the treatment of tu). This will often find in some menus and dialog boxes, and especially in settings (if other than dialog boxes where the computer gives us some information or ask us something.). This will not use Editar but Edita or Obrir but Obre to express the imperative. As in Catalan (unlike what is done in English) orders are expressed by a verb ending specifically suitable for this, we must translate these statements in formulating them as imperative. So, under no circumstances use the infinitive (in Catalan does not apply to express orders), nor a way to switch between one form and the other (because the mix of verbal forms undertakes the necessary linguistic homogeneity in any language technical or specialized).
    • The computer addresses the user: When the computer is addressed to the user to give information, make him a question, etc., the form to use is the second person plural imperative (which corresponds to the treatment of vós). This is mainly found in the documentation and in some dialog boxes. The use of second person plural does not necessarily mean that the computer is being addressed to more than one person, so do not write esteu desconnectats but esteu desconnectat or esteu segurs but esteu segur.

Relationships names

For the relationships names should be taken of the Catalan Society of Genealogy, Heraldry, Sigillography, Vexillology and Nobility. This causes any problems when the translation program uses variables (eg. great x6 grandfather ís avi 7è, but great x%d grandfather is avi %d+1 è).
