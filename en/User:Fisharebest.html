I'm Greg Roach, and I'm the leader of the webtrees project. Before starting webtrees, I was leader of the PhpGedView project, and have a long history of running open-source projects.

I've been writing software pretty much full-time since I saved up my paper-round money to buy a ZX80 kit and a soldering iron, some 30 years ago.

Currently I work at the Institute of Educational Technology, at the Open University in England.

When I'm not sat in front of a keyboard, I'm keen on scuba-diving, running and the great outdoors.


Thoughts on webtrees 2.0

  • Instead of storing GEDCOM data and repeatedly parsing it, we should store data in a normalised form that is **directly convertible** to / from GEDCOM. This means that there needs to be somewhere to store "other" data - that is to say unrecognised GEDCOM tags - which can be added back when the data is exported.
  • A common cause of GEDCOM errors are mismatched INDI-FAM links. We should only store one copy of each of these, and generate the reverse link on export.
  • A common cause of GEDCOM errors is case-insentivity of XREFs. We should be case-sensitive, and prevent invalid links from being entered.
